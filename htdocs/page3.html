New Internet Slang: Understanding the New Generation

Common internet slang

Ratio

Ratio refers to the ratio of replies to retweets or likes in a Twitter post. It can also refer to the ratio of likes or retweets of a reply or a quote retweet to the likes or retweets of the former post. If this ratio is significantly great, like 2:1 or greater, the opnion the poster wants to make, the poster, or the post, is undermined, and the post is considered "ratio'd".

L and W

L is a shorted form of "loss". It is used in situations of unfortunate outcome. Used by itself, it means "Take the L", or "Take the loss", which is used for poking fun at people for losing a game, or suffering a fate well-deserved. In contrast, W means "win".

About tone indicators

Tone indicators are expressions used at the end of any text message or post that convey tones that wouldn't be normally conveyed in a text-only environment like the internet. Many people claim that tone indicators help them, as well as the neurodivergent, or people with neurological deviations such as autism and dyslexia. Tone indicators are part of a group of beginning linguistic trends related to accessibility and vulerability (for example, trigger warnings and sharing of pronouns). Tone indicators fare the best among some communities of juveniles in Tumblr and Twitter and common among the anime and K-pop fandoms, young teen aesthetic, and those with serious consideration for mental health and gender issues. They were created and used to promote inclusitivity and aid other users in having more convenient experiences on the internet.[1]
